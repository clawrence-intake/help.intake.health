## About These Templates

These templates are based on real support exchanges handled by Michael Bender at Intake Health. They reflect the actual tone, structure, and resolution patterns used with customers including Minnesota Twins, University of Michigan, Tampa Bay Buccaneers, Chelsea FC, and others.

**Tone guidelines:**
- Brief and friendly — never overly formal or long
- Empathetic but efficient ("Sorry for the trouble")
- Quick to offer concrete next steps rather than prolonged back-and-forth
- Always end with an offer to help further or a clear next action

## Template 4: Moisture Confirmed — Next Steps

**When to use:** Customer confirms cloudy/foggy window on back of device.

**Subject:** Re: InFlow Issue

---

Thanks — the cloudy appearance means humidity has built up inside the device. That's what's causing the problem.

Here's what I'd recommend trying first — it often brings units back fully:

1. Remove from urinal and clean with mild soap and a soft cloth. **Do not charge yet.**
2. Leave out in a dry room for **24 hours** to air dry fully.
3. Place in a ziplock bag with a desiccant packet (silica gel), seal it completely, and leave for **72 hours**.
4. After 72 hours, charge normally — you should see the red charging light come on.

If you don't have desiccant packets handy, I can send some along with some fresh units. Let me know how many urinals you need to support and your shipping address.
