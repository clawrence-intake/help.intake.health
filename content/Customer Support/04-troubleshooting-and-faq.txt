## Quick Troubleshooting Guide

### InFlow won't charge / no red light on charger

**Symptoms:** Device doesn't illuminate red when placed on charger, tried multiple chargers and outlets with no success.

**Step 1 — Check for moisture damage first.**
Look through the **clear window on the back** of the InFlow Cartridge. If you see cloudiness, water droplets, or a foggy appearance inside the plastic, humidity has built up internally and is preventing charging. This is the most common cause in units 12+ months old. Perform the humidity maintenance process (see `03-charging-and-maintenance.md`) before anything else.

**Step 2 — Check charging connection.**
Ensure you're using the InFlow-provided charging base plugged directly into a wall outlet (not a USB port). Remove InFlow from the charger, wait 8 seconds, and reconnect. A solid red light should appear.

**Step 3 — Check charge history.**
Has this unit charged successfully before? If yes and it's now unresponsive after trying the above steps, it likely needs a warranty replacement — contact support@intake.health.

> **How to diagnose moisture damage:** Hold the cartridge up to a light source and look through the clear lens window on the back. A **clear, undistorted view** is normal. A **cloudy, foggy, or visibly wet** interior means moisture has accumulated and is likely causing the failure.

### InFlow is not running tests / no light comes on when urine is added

**Most likely causes:**
1. **Battery is dead.** Check if InFlow flashes blue when disturbed. If so, charge it.
2. **Not installed vertically.** InFlow must be mounted vertically so the cup fills and urine contacts the sensor pin. Recheck the mount angle.
3. **Cup is blocked or not filling.** Make sure nothing is obstructing the cup opening.
4. **Suction cups have lost grip and InFlow shifted.** Re-run suction cups under hot water and re-mount.

**Fix:** Confirm vertical install, charge if needed, and re-mount with fresh suction cup adhesion.

---

### InFlow is flashing white lights

**Cause:** Device error — this can be caused by dropping InFlow, internal moisture/leak, or another malfunction.

**Fix:** Contact Intake Health support at support@intake.health. Do not attempt to open or repair the device.

---

### InFlow flashes or does not illuminate steadily while on the charger

**Cause:** Poor wireless connection between the cartridge and the charging base.

**Fix:**
1. Remove InFlow from the charger.
2. Wait 8 seconds.
3. Reconnect InFlow to the charger.
4. Repeat until you see a solid red illumination (indicating it is charging properly).
5. If the issue persists, ensure you are using the InFlow-provided charging base plugged directly into a wall outlet — not a USB port or non-provided adapter.

---

### InFlow suction cups are not staying on the urinal

**Cause:** Suction cups dry out and lose adhesion over time.

**Fix:** Run the suction cups under hot water for 30–60 seconds to make them soft and malleable, then press InFlow firmly against the urinal for several seconds. This usually restores adhesion. If suction cups are cracked or damaged, contact support for replacement parts.

---

### InFlow seems to be giving inaccurate results

**Possible causes and explanations:**

- **B vitamins / multivitamins:** B vitamins make urine unusually bright yellow, which can cause InFlow to read as yellow or red even when the person is adequately hydrated. This is normal — educate users to avoid taking multivitamins right before a test, or to factor this in when interpreting results. Intake Health is developing AI-based algorithms to compensate for this.

- **Certain medications:** Some chronic medications (particularly thyroid medications) can alter urine color or composition. Individuals on these medications may consistently read yellow or red regardless of actual hydration. This is a known limitation — consider using a urine refractometer for these individuals as a supplement.

- **Menstrual blood:** Can affect optical readings. Users who are menstruating may choose to skip the test on those days.

- **Just drank a lot of water:** Hydration takes 12–24 hours to build up in the body. Drinking a large amount of water right before a test will not produce a green result. This is normal physiology, not a device error — and it's actually one of the most valuable lessons InFlow teaches: consistent hydration matters more than last-minute chugging.

- **Unit needs humidity maintenance:** If a unit has been in a humid environment for an extended period without maintenance, internal moisture can affect sensor performance. Perform the humidity maintenance process (see `03-charging-and-maintenance.md`).

---

### Battery life seems shorter than expected

**Cause:** Internal humidity buildup is the most common cause of reduced battery life in InFlow. Humidity permeates the plastic housing over time and degrades battery performance.

**Fix:** Perform the humidity maintenance process using the desiccant kit. This can often fully restore battery life. See `03-charging-and-maintenance.md` for step-by-step instructions.

---

### An InFlow that seemed "dead" won't revive

**Try the humidity maintenance process first.** Many units that appear dead are actually suffering from moisture buildup and can be fully revived with the desiccant kit treatment.

If the unit remains unresponsive after humidity maintenance and a full charge attempt, contact support@intake.health.

---

### InFlow is stuck on a solid white light and won't do anything else

**Cause:** A device locked on a constant (non-flashing) white light is in an error state — typically caused by an internal fault triggered during or after charging. This is distinct from flashing white lights (which also indicate an error but may be caused by a drop or impact).

**Fix:** If the device has been working previously and is now stuck on solid white, this usually requires a warranty replacement. Contact support@intake.health with your name, organization, and shipping address. Intake Health will send a replacement and include a prepaid return label and ziplock bag for sending the faulty unit back.

> *Real example: A customer called in reporting their InFlow had been stuck on solid white for a week after taking it out to charge. The device could not run tests at all. This was resolved with a warranty replacement.*

---

### What cleaners can I use near InFlow?

- ✅ **Safe:** Water (rinse weekly), non-HCL-based cleaners (rinse InFlow immediately after)
- ❌ **Not safe:** Any cleaner containing **hydrochloric acid (HCL)** — found in many commercial toilet bowl cleaners. Do not use chemical cleaners directly on the cartridge lens or sensor pin.
- ❌ **Not safe:** Stiff-bristle brushes on the cartridge

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General

**Q: What does InFlow measure?**
A: InFlow measures urine concentration using optical spectroscopy — essentially analyzing how light interacts with the urine in the cup. This gives an accurate real-time read of hydration status, displayed as green (well hydrated), yellow (mildly dehydrated), or red (significantly dehydrated). Future versions will also detect sodium, potassium, and other biomarkers.

**Q: Is InFlow a medical device?**
A: No. InFlow is a wellness and performance monitoring tool. It is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease. Information from InFlow should not replace diagnostic testing or consultation with a medical provider.

**Q: Is there an app?**
A: The current InFlow device provides instant visual feedback via LED light only — there is no companion app required for basic use. Contact Intake Health for information on data integration and reporting options for your organization.

**Q: Does InFlow work for female athletes?**
A: The current InFlow is designed for urinals, but many athletic trainers use it successfully with female athletes by placing it in a sink or bathtub with a cup. A dedicated toilet-mounted version (InLoo) is in development. Contact support@intake.health for updates.

### Installation & Setup

**Q: How do I get InFlow to stick properly in the urinal?**
A: Run the suction cups under hot water for 30 seconds before mounting. This softens the suction cups and creates a much stronger seal. Press InFlow firmly against the back of the urinal about 6 inches above the drain and hold for a few seconds.

**Q: Does InFlow need to be connected to power or Wi-Fi?**
A: No. InFlow is completely wireless and battery-powered. No wiring, no Wi-Fi connection, and no installation by facilities staff is needed. It runs entirely on its internal rechargeable battery.

**Q: Does it require any plumbing modifications?**
A: No. InFlow attaches to the inside of any standard urinal using suction cups. It requires no plumbing, drilling, or permanent modifications.

**Q: How many InFlows do I need?**
A: One per urinal for full coverage. Most facilities start with enough units to cover all urinals in the primary locker room or restroom and expand from there. Common starter configurations are 4 units (one 4-pack) or 8 units (two 4-packs).

### Battery & Charging

**Q: How long does the battery last?**
A: InFlow delivers approximately 4,000 tests per charge. In a busy locker room, that typically works out to every 3 to 8 weeks between charges.

**Q: How long does it take to charge?**
A: Approximately 2 hours for a full charge.

**Q: Do I need to take InFlow off the urinal to charge it?**
A: No. The InFlow Cup (the part with suction cups) stays mounted on the urinal. You only remove the InFlow Cartridge (the sensor module), which detaches magnetically. You can charge the cartridge and then snap it back in without disturbing the mount.

**Q: What charger should I use?**
A: Only the InFlow-provided wireless charging base and adapter plug. Plug it directly into a wall outlet. Do not connect it to a USB port on a computer or a third-party charging adapter.

**Q: What happens if a unit dies in the middle of the day?**
A: InFlow will flash blue to warn you before the battery fully runs out. Once it dies, it simply stops running tests until recharged. It does not affect the operation of other units. For 24-hour facilities, keeping a spare charged unit on hand ensures continuous coverage.

### Maintenance

**Q: Does InFlow require a lot of maintenance?**
A: Not much. You'll charge it every 3–8 weeks and do a humidity reset every 3–6 months. Both are simple and take minimal hands-on time. If you prefer zero maintenance, consider the lease program — fresh units are swapped in on a regular schedule.

**Q: My unit has been in a hot locker room all season. Can I revive it?**
A: Very likely yes. Humidity buildup is the most common cause of degraded performance. The humidity maintenance process using the desiccant kit can revive units that have been in humid environments for extended periods, even ones that appear dead. See `03-charging-and-maintenance.md`.

**Q: How often should I do humidity maintenance in a really humid environment?**
A: Every 3 months for hot locker rooms, shower areas, outdoor platforms, or anywhere with poor ventilation. Standard indoor environments: every 6 months.

### Hydration Results

**Q: Why did my athletes all get red results right after a road trip where they were drinking lots of water?**
A: Check whether your athletes were taking multivitamins or B-complex supplements before testing. B vitamins cause unusually bright yellow urine, which can trigger yellow or red results even in well-hydrated individuals. Have athletes avoid vitamins for a few hours before testing, or factor this in when interpreting results.

**Q: One of my athletes always gets red no matter how much they drink. Why?**
A: This is most commonly caused by chronic medication — particularly thyroid medication — that alters urine color. InFlow isn't broken; the medication is creating a reading it can't compensate for with the current version. For athletes on chronic medication, consider using a urine refractometer as a supplement to InFlow. Intake Health is working on AI-driven algorithms to handle this in future versions.

**Q: Can athletes cheat the test by chugging water right before?**
A: Yes — and it won't help them. Drinking a large amount of water immediately before a test will not produce a green result, because it takes 12–24 hours for hydration to show up in urine. This is actually one of InFlow's best teaching moments: when an athlete says "I drank so much water and still got yellow," you can explain that what their urine shows right now reflects what they did yesterday and this morning — not the water they just drank.

**Q: Does InFlow work as a constant educational tool, or only during formal testing sessions?**
A: Both. InFlow is always on and always ready, which means every time someone visits the restroom they get feedback. This is one of InFlow's biggest advantages over manual testing: the daily, passive exposure builds consistent hydration habits over time, not just compliance on test days. Customers consistently report that hydration levels across their whole team improve simply because people start paying attention every day.

### Product Longevity

**Q: How long do InFlow units last?**
A: There are no consumable parts — InFlow doesn't wear out on a set schedule. Most customers get 2+ seasons of use per unit. The main reasons units eventually need replacement are the harsh restroom environment (units get dropped, dirty, or damaged) rather than device failure. Budget for replacing a portion of your units annually, but not a full replacement.

**Q: Are there any replacement parts?**
